Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about therapy, insurance, and what to expect.

Getting Started

You can contact us by filling out our online contact form, calling 516-336-9209, or emailing admin@pathwayswithinwisdom.com. We'll get back to you within 1-2 business days to discuss your needs and schedule your consultation.
Your first session (often called an intake or assessment) is an opportunity for your therapist to get to know you. They'll ask about what brings you to therapy, relevant history, and your goals. You'll also have time to ask questions and get a sense of whether the therapist is a good fit. Together, you'll discuss what you hope to achieve and develop a preliminary plan.
Therapy can benefit anyone, not just those in crisis. Common signs that therapy might help include: persistent feelings of sadness or anxiety, difficulty managing stress, relationship problems, major life transitions, trauma or grief, feeling "stuck," or simply wanting personal growth. If you're unsure, a consultation can help determine if therapy is right for you.
Finding the right fit is important. Consider what you're looking for in a therapist—their specializations, approach, personality, and availability. When you contact us, we'll help match you with a clinician based on your needs. It's okay to try a session and decide if it feels right; we're happy to help you find another option if needed.

Appointments & Sessions

Session frequency varies based on your needs and goals. Most clients start with weekly sessions. As you progress, sessions may become biweekly or monthly. Your therapist will work with you to determine the best frequency for your situation.
Individual therapy sessions are typically 45-50 minutes. Couples and family sessions are often 50-90 minutes depending on the format. Your first session may be slightly longer to allow for intake paperwork and assessment.
Yes! We offer secure video therapy sessions through a HIPAA-compliant platform. Many clients find telehealth convenient and just as effective as in-person sessions. You can choose in-person, telehealth, or a combination based on your preferences.
We require 24-hour notice for cancellations. Late cancellations or no-shows may be subject to a fee. We understand emergencies happen—please communicate with us and we'll do our best to accommodate your needs.
The length of therapy varies widely depending on your goals and concerns. Some clients achieve their goals in a few months; others benefit from longer-term support. Therapy is not meant to last forever—the goal is to help you develop skills and insight so you can eventually manage on your own.

Insurance & Payment

Yes, we accept many major insurance plans including Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna, United Healthcare, Oxford, and Empire. Insurance coverage varies, so we recommend contacting us to verify your specific benefits before your first appointment.
If we're not in-network with your plan, we can provide a superbill (detailed receipt) that you can submit to your insurance for out-of-network reimbursement. Many plans offer partial reimbursement for out-of-network mental health services. We also offer competitive self-pay rates and sliding scale options for those who qualify.
Costs vary depending on your insurance coverage. For those using insurance, you'll typically pay your copay or coinsurance amount. Self-pay rates vary by therapist and service type. We're happy to discuss fees during your initial contact. Don't let cost be a barrier—ask about sliding scale options if needed.
Payment (copay, coinsurance, or self-pay amount) is due at the time of service. We accept credit cards, debit cards, and HSA/FSA cards.

Confidentiality & Privacy

Yes, what you share in therapy is confidential. Your therapist will not share information about you without your written consent, except in specific situations required by law: if there's risk of harm to yourself or others, suspected abuse of a child or vulnerable adult, or if records are subpoenaed by a court. Your therapist will discuss confidentiality limits during your first session.
Under HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act), you have the right to access your records, request corrections, and know how your information is used. We take privacy seriously and follow all legal requirements to protect your information. You'll receive our full Notice of Privacy Practices before your first session.
Only with your written consent. Coordinated care can be beneficial, but you decide who can communicate about your treatment. You can authorize (or limit) communication with specific providers at any time.

About Therapy

We offer a range of evidence-based approaches including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), EMDR for trauma, psychodynamic therapy, play therapy for children, Gottman Method couples therapy, and more. Your therapist will work with you to determine the best approach for your needs.
Therapy helps you develop insight, skills, and strategies for managing challenges through conversation and structured exercises. Medication can help manage symptoms by affecting brain chemistry. Many people benefit from one or the other; some benefit from both together. We offer psychiatric medication management in addition to therapy if needed.
Yes, our psychiatric nurse practitioners can provide evaluation and medication management for mental health conditions. This is often done in coordination with your therapist for comprehensive care. Ask us about adding medication management to your treatment if you're interested.
For non-urgent matters, you can leave a message and your therapist will respond within 1-2 business days. For urgent situations, please use crisis resources: call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), text HOME to 741741, or call 911 for emergencies. Your therapist will discuss between-session support during your first meeting.
