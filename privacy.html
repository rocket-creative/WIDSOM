Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us.

Effective Date: January 1, 2024

Introduction

Pathways Within Wisdom ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website and use our services.

Information We Collect

Personal Information

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us, including:

  • Name, email address, phone number
  • Address and demographic information
  • Insurance information
  • Information provided in contact forms

Health Information

As a mental health practice, we collect protected health information (PHI) as necessary to provide treatment. This information is protected under HIPAA and includes:

  • Medical and mental health history
  • Treatment records and session notes
  • Diagnoses and treatment plans

Note: PHI is handled according to our separate Notice of Privacy Practices, which you will receive before beginning treatment.

Website Usage Information

We may automatically collect certain information when you visit our website:

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring website
  • Device information

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve our services
  • Respond to your inquiries and schedule appointments
  • Process insurance claims and payments
  • Send appointment reminders and communications
  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Protect our rights and the safety of others

Disclosure of Your Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information in the following circumstances:

  • With your consent: When you authorize disclosure to other providers or parties
  • For treatment: With other healthcare providers involved in your care (with consent)
  • For payment: With insurance companies to process claims
  • As required by law: When legally obligated, such as court orders or mandatory reporting
  • To protect safety: When there is risk of harm to yourself or others

HIPAA Compliance

As a healthcare provider, we comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). Your protected health information receives the highest level of protection. You have rights under HIPAA to:

  • Access your health records
  • Request corrections to your records
  • Receive an accounting of disclosures
  • Request restrictions on certain uses
  • File a complaint if you believe your rights have been violated

Our complete Notice of Privacy Practices is provided to all clients before treatment begins.

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including:

  • Encrypted data transmission (SSL/TLS)
  • Secure electronic health records systems
  • Staff training on privacy practices
  • Physical security measures
  • Access controls and authentication

However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal information
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Opt out of marketing communications
  • Withdraw consent for data processing

Cookies and Tracking

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience. You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies.

Children's Privacy

Our services include treatment for children and adolescents. For clients under 18, we obtain consent from parents or guardians and comply with applicable laws regarding minors' health information.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
